Proof of AA Meeting Attendance for Probation

If you're on probation with a meeting requirement, your probation officer needs three things: which meetings you attended (name, date, time, location), a running total against your required count (e.g. 3 meetings per week for 12 months), and a reason to trust the record. Paper attendance cards cover the first two and rely on a chair's signature for the third — and they get lost, smudged, and questioned.

A MyRecoveryPal attendance report covers all three in one PDF: every logged meeting with full details, a summary against your court's weekly requirement, and a SHA-256 fingerprint printed on the document that your PO can check at our public verification page in ten seconds. Self-reported entries are clearly flagged as such — the report never overstates what it can prove, which is exactly why POs can take it seriously.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the court verify AA meeting attendance?

Courts traditionally verify AA or NA attendance with a paper card signed by the meeting chair, which your probation officer reviews at check-ins. Increasingly, courts and POs also accept digital attendance logs. MyRecoveryPal generates a PDF attendance report with a tamper-evident SHA-256 fingerprint and a public verification URL, so your probation officer can independently confirm the report hasn't been altered — something a paper card can't offer. Always confirm with your PO which formats your court accepts.

Does this work for DUI classes and drug court programs?

Yes. The report covers any date range, so it works for a 90-day DUI meeting requirement, a multi-year drug court program, or family court orders. Note that DUI education classes (state-certified programs) issue their own completion certificates — MyRecoveryPal documents the recovery-meeting attendance portion of your requirements (AA, NA, SMART, and other programs).

Will my court accept a PDF report instead of a signed paper card?

Most courts now accept digital documentation, especially during/after COVID. The tamper-evident fingerprint and verification URL make this report stronger evidence than a paper card — and probation officers can confirm authenticity instantly. We recommend confirming with your specific PO that they accept email submissions before relying solely on this. Self-reported entries are clearly flagged in the report.

What if my court requires meeting chair signatures?

Phase 1 supports typed-name chair signatures with a timestamp. If your court requires a physical signature from the meeting secretary, we recommend continuing to use a paper attendance card alongside this for now — and use MyRecoveryPal for the cumulative report and verification. Cryptographic chair signatures and QR check-in are on the roadmap.

Can I use this for SMART Recovery or other secular programs?

Yes. Courts cannot constitutionally require 12-step-only attendance (per multiple appellate rulings). MyRecoveryPal tracks AA, NA, CA, MA, GA, SMART Recovery, Refuge Recovery, LifeRing, and other recovery programs equally.

Is this a substitute for actually attending meetings?

No. This is a record-keeping tool. Self-reported entries are clearly flagged. Misrepresenting attendance to a court is perjury — please don't.

How is my information protected?

Your case number and probation officer's email are visible only to you and (when you choose) the people you email reports to. The public verify page intentionally does not show your name, case number, or any personal information — only that a report with that fingerprint exists.
