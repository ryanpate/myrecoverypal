The Tradition of the Sobriety Medallion

The sobriety medallion — also called an AA chip, sober coin, or recovery token — has been a cornerstone of 12-step recovery since the 1940s. Physical medallions are handed out at meetings to mark milestones: 24 hours, 30 days, 60 days, 90 days, six months, nine months, and every year after. They're a tangible reminder that clean time is hard-won and worth celebrating.

A digital medallion carries the same symbolic weight — without having to wait for Thursday's meeting. Share it the moment you hit day 30. Set it as your lockscreen at 90 days. Post your one-year coin on Instagram so your friends can cheer you on. Every day of recovery deserves to be marked, and you don't need a program membership to do it.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a sobriety medallion?

A sobriety medallion (also called an AA chip, recovery coin, or sober token) is a physical or digital marker that commemorates a specific amount of clean time. Medallions are traditionally handed out at AA meetings at milestones like 24 hours, 30, 60, and 90 days, then 6 months, 9 months, and every year after.

Why do year medallions use Roman numerals?

Roman numerals on year medallions is an AA tradition dating back decades. Physical AA medallions stamp the year count (V for 5, X for 10, XXV for 25) to match the classical feel of the coin. Our digital medallions follow the same convention — year badges display the Roman numeral only, while month and day badges show the number with the unit label underneath.

Can I share my medallion on Instagram, TikTok, or Facebook?

What's the difference between a medallion, chip, and sober coin?

All three terms refer to the same thing — a token marking continuous clean time in recovery. AA typically calls them medallions or chips, NA calls them keytags, and SMART Recovery calls them anniversary tokens. Our maker produces digital versions that work as memorabilia regardless of which program you follow.
