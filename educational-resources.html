Resources for People in Recovery

Websites & Online Communities

  • SMART Recovery – Evidence-based tools & meetings (online & in-person).
  • In The Rooms – Global online meetings, groups, webinars.
  • Recovery Dharma – Buddhist-inspired peer support & step work.
  • Sober Grid – Mobile social network for milestones & 24/7 support.
  • This Naked Mind – Articles, free resources & paid courses to rewire drinking habits.

Podcasts

  • The Bubble Hour – Weekly stories & discussions on sobriety challenges.
  • Recovery Elevator – Interviews with experts & lived-experience insights.
  • Harmony Foundation Podcast – Recovery tips, guest speakers, meditation sessions.

YouTube Channels

  • The Recovery Elevator – Video interviews, tips & livestream meetings.
  • The Sober Guy – Personal vlogs on life in long-term recovery.
  • TED Talks: Addiction & Recovery – Curated talks on brain science & recovery journeys.

Books

  • “Alcoholics Anonymous” (The Big Book) – Foundational text of 12-step recovery.
  • “The Unexpected Joy of Being Sober” by Catherine Gray – Personal memoir & strategies.
  • “The Recovery Book” by Al J. Mooney & Catherine Dold – Evidence-based guide & timeline.

Other Media & Tools

  • “The Anonymous People” (Documentary) – Profiles of recovery advocates.
  • “Recovery Boys” (Documentary) – Five men navigate early recovery.
  • “I Am Sober” App – Daily tracking, community badges & reminders.

Resources for Friends & Family

Websites & Support Groups

  • Al-Anon Family Groups – Literature & meetings for loved ones of drinkers.
  • Nar-Anon Family Groups – Peer support for families affected by addiction.
  • SAMHSA Family Resources – Guides, toolkits & webinars.
  • NAMI Family-to-Family – Free 8-week education program.

Podcasts

  • Al-Anon Podcast Series – Stories & tips for family recovery.
  • Nar-Anon Voices Podcast – Member stories & coping strategies.

YouTube Channels

  • Al-Anon YouTube Channel – Talks, Q&A’s & recovery education.
  • TEDx Talks: Addiction Impact – Presentations on family & community effects.

Books

  • “Codependent No More” by Melody Beattie – Healing codependency workbook.
  • “Beyond Addiction” by Jeffrey Foote – Compassionate approach for loved ones.
  • “Perfect Daughters” by Robert Lindner – Understanding addiction’s family ripple effects.

Other Media & Tools

  • “The Anonymous People” (Documentary) – Profiles of those advocating for recovery.
  • Psychology Today: Substance Use – Articles on addiction & family dynamics.
  • “Al-Anon Meetings” App – Find local & online meetings, meeting schedules.
